# APA 7th Edition Citation Guide

This guide provides a summary of essential APA 7th edition citation guidelines for this project.

## In-Text Citations

### General Format
*   **Parenthetical citation**: (Author, Year)
    *   Example: (Smith, 2020)
*   **Narrative citation**: Author (Year)
    *   Example: Smith (2020) argued...

### Multiple Authors
*   **Two authors**: (Author1 & Author2, Year)
    *   Example: (Smith & Jones, 2021)
*   **Three or more authors**: (Author et al., Year)
    *   Example: (Brown et al., 2019)

### Specific Parts of a Source
*   Include page numbers for direct quotes: (Author, Year, p. X) or (Author, Year, pp. X-Y)
    *   Example: (Smith, 2020, p. 45)

## Reference List

*   Start on a new page, titled "References" (centered, bold).
*   Double-space all entries.
*   Use a hanging indent for each entry (first line flush left, subsequent lines indented).
*   Order entries alphabetically by the first author's last name.

### Common Reference Formats

#### Journal Article
Author, A. A., Author, B. B., & Author, C. C. (Year). Title of article. *Title of Periodical, volume*(issue), pages. DOI

*   Example:
    *   Light, M. A., & Light, I. H. (2008). The geographic distribution of crime. *Journal of Quantitative Criminology, 24*(1), 1–21. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10940-007-9031-8

#### Book
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of work*. Publisher.

*   Example:
    *   Sapolsky, R. M. (2017). *Behave: The biology of humans at our best and worst*. Penguin Books.

#### Chapter in an Edited Book
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of chapter. In E. E. Editor & F. F. Editor (Eds.), *Title of work* (pp. pages). Publisher.

*   Example:
    *   Aron, L., Botella, M., & Lubart, T. (2019). Culinary arts: Talent and its development. In R. F. Subotnik, P. Olszewski-Kubilius, & F. J. Worrell (Eds.), *The psychology of high performance: Developing human potential into productivit*y (pp. 345–371). American Psychological Association.

#### Website/Webpage
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). *Title of page*. Site name. URL

*   Example:
    *   American Psychological Association. (n.d.). *APA style*. Retrieved Month Day, Year, from https://apastyle.apa.org/

#### Report
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of report* (Report No. XXX). Publisher.

*   Example:
    *   Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development. (2018). *The future of education and skills: Education 2030*. OECD Publishing. https://www.oecd.org/education/2030/E2030%20Position%20Paper%20(05.04.2018).pdf
